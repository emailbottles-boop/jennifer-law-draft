21+ Years • Thousands of Clients

Jennifer Stewart was born in Puyallup, Washington, and spent much of her early life in Thurston and Lewis Counties. As a single mother in the early 1990s, she began her academic journey at Centralia Community College, later attending Yakima Community College, and ultimately earning her Associate of Arts degree from Centralia. In 1996, she relocated to the Seattle area and was admitted to the University of Washington in 1998.

While attending the University of Washington, Jennifer worked as a law clerk at Stewart Beall & MacNichols P.S., gaining early hands-on legal experience. In 2000, she earned her Bachelor of Science degree in Speech and Hearing Sciences. After careful consideration, she chose an alternative path to licensure by enrolling in the Washington State Bar Association’s Rule Six Law Clerk Program. She was initially mentored by Stephen Rochon, then a Municipal Court Judge for the cities of Pacific and Maple Valley, and later completed the program under the guidance of Scott Stewart.

During the Rule Six Program, Jennifer was authorized to engage in the limited practice of law as a Rule Nine Legal Intern. In this role, she served as a public defender in multiple jurisdictions, including Kent, Kenmore, North Bend, Sammamish, and Lakewood. During this period, and prior to taking the bar exam, she successfully tried four of five criminal jury trials.

Jennifer passed the Washington State Bar Exam on her first attempt in August 2006 and was admitted to practice law in early 2007. Since that time, she has built a reputation as a tenacious and highly prepared advocate, known for her strategic legal analysis and willingness to take challenging cases to trial. She approaches every case with personal dedication and an unwavering commitment to achieving the best possible outcome for her clients.

Throughout her career, Jennifer has represented thousands of individuals in Municipal, District, and Superior Courts throughout King and Pierce Counties, as well as courts across Washington State. Her experience spans a broad range of criminal matters, from misdemeanor offenses to serious felony charges, reflecting more than two decades of dedicated service to fairness, justice, and effective defense advocacy.
